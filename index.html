México Trivia: Did you know?

Mexico has 10-12% of the world’s biodiversity, making it the fourth most biodiverse country in the world. The wildlife includes tourist favorites like spider monkeys, ocelots, and macaws.

México Trivia: Did you know?

Chocolate originated in Mexico. The Aztecs and Mayans first cultivated the cacao plant thousands of years ago. They typically enjoyed it as a drink, and they used the beans as currency.

México Trivia: Did you know?

Mexico is known for its beautiful cenotes . Cenotes, or natural deep water pits and caves, are a popular swimming spot for tourists and have a particular significance for the Mayan people, who used them as water sources.

México Trivia: Did you know?

Mexico's Independence Day is celebrated on September 16th. September 16, 1821 marks the beginning of The Mexico War of Independence that ultimately ended Spanish rule over Mexico.

México Trivia: Did you know?

Dia de los Muertos is a Mexican holiday that celebrates the dead. Before colonization, it was celebrated in the summer, but now it falls around the same time as Halloween.

México Trivia: Did you know?

México has no official language. While most Mexicans speak Spanish, there are 68 recognized languages in the country, mostly indigenous. Four of these are language isolates, or languages that don’t relate to any other language.

México Trivia: Did you know?

The Great Pyramid of Cholula is the largest pyramid in the world. Larger than The Great Pyramid of Giza, the Great Pyramid of Cholula is said to have been constructed for the Aztec god Quetzalcoatl.

México Trivia: Did you know?

Frida Kahlo was born just outside of Mexico City in 1907. Today, she is one of Mexico's most famous artists, known for her fantastical self-portraits.
